Welcome to Germany and the Olympics

Athletes from Germany have taken part in most of the modern Olympic Games held since 1896. Germany has hosted three Olympic Games, in 1936 both the Winter and Summer Games, and the 1972 Summer Olympics. In addition, Germany had been selected to host the 1916 Summer Olympics as well as the 1940 Winter Olympics, both of which had to be cancelled due to World Wars. After these wars, Germany was banned from participating in the 1920, 1924 and 1948 Olympics.

While the country was divided, each of the two German states boycotted one of the Summer Games. In 1980, West Germany was one of 66 nations which did not go to Moscow in protest at the Soviet Union's invasion of Afghanistan, and in 1984 East Germany joined the Soviet Union and several others in the boycott of the Summer Games in Los Angeles. In 1990, East Germany and West Germany would reunite, with Germany once again competing as a single full sovereign state since the 1992 Olympic year.

The IOC currently splits German results among four codes, even though only the German Democratic Republic (GDR) from 1968 to 1988 had sent a separate team to compete against the team of the German NOC that represented Germany (GER) since 1896.

Germany's Achievements in the Olympics

Germany has a long and successful history in the Olympic Games, consistently ranking among the top nations in terms of medal counts. Here are some key achievements and highlights:

  • Over 1,700 medals in Summer and Winter Games
    • Overall Performance: Germany has won over 1,000 medals in the Summer Olympics, making it one of the most successful countries in Olympic history.
    • Top Sports: Germany has excelled in athletics, swimming, rowing, cycling, equestrian events, and team sports like football and hockey.
    • Notable Moments: In the 2016 Rio Olympics, Germany finished 5th in the medal table with 17 gold medals. In the 2020 Tokyo Olympics (held in 2021), Germany won 10 gold medals and finished 9th overall. East Germany (GDR) was particularly dominant in the 1970s and 1980s, especially in swimming and athletics, before reunification in 1990.

Famous German Olympians

Many German athletes have made their mark in Olympic history. Here are some of the most famous:

    Michael Groß

    Michael Groß is a former German swimmer known for his dominance in the 1980s, Swimmer with multiple gold medals earning the nickname "The Albatross" due to his long arms and powerful strokes.

    Katarina Witt

    Katarina Witt is a former German figure skater and two-time Olympic gold medalist (1984, 1988), known for her artistic performances and technical excellence.Witt became a global icon, winning multiple World championships.

    Birgit Fischer

    Birgit Fischer Legendary German canoeist and the most successful Olympic canoeist of all time, winning eight gold medals across six Olympic Games. She dominated sprint canoeing, excelling in both individual and team events.

    Thomas Bach Named Honorary President for Life

    On March 19, 2025, the IOC named outgoing president Thomas Bach honorary president for life. A former Olympic fencing champion, Bach led since 2013, implementing reforms to cut hosting costs. His tenure faced challenges like the Russian doping scandal, Korean tensions, and COVID-19, which delayed the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. He steps down in June.

    Germany's Performance at the Paris 2024 Olympics

    Germany finished 10th at the Paris 2024 Olympics, winning 33 medals—its worst performance since reunification. The German Olympic Committee cited outdated sports infrastructure and fragmented governance as key issues. In response, plans include raising the sports budget to €331 million by 2025 and creating a central agency to oversee elite sports.

    Bayern Munich Players Honored as Germany's Players of the Year

    In December 2024, Bayern Munich stars Jamal Musiala and Giulia Gwinn were named Germany’s Players of the Year. Musiala was EURO 2024’s joint top scorer and key in the Nations League. Gwinn shined with eight goals, helping Germany win Olympic and Nations League bronze, scoring a crucial goal against Spain.

    Germany's Women's Football Team Secures Bronze

    Germany’s women’s football team won the bronze medal at the Paris 2024 Olympics, defeating Spain 1-0. Goalkeeper Ann-Katrin Berger made key saves to keep a clean sheet, while Giulia Gwinn secured victory with a decisive second-half penalty. The win marked a strong finish for Germany, highlighting their resilience and determination.

    Germany's Men's Hockey Team at Paris 2024

    Germany’s men’s hockey team had a remarkable run at the Paris 2024 Olympics, showcasing skill and determination. They reached the semifinals, where they edged past India 3-2 in a thrilling contest. The hard-fought victory secured their place in the final against the Netherlands, as they aimed to claim Olympic gold and cement their legacy.

    Germany Advances Bid to Host 2040 Olympic Games

    Germany is actively pursuing a bid to host the 2040 Olympic and Paralympic Games. The Federal Cabinet approved a joint declaration with the German Olympic Sports Confederation (DOSB) and participating cities and federal states, reflecting unanimous support for the submission. The bid emphasizes sustainability and aims to mark 50 years since German reunification.
